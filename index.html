Projection rules 2026–2030

  • Fleet: where an official national 2030 target exists (Germany: 15 M, Masterplan Ladeinfrastruktur II) the path interpolates geometrically to it, then the Adoption-Speed slider scales it 0.5–1.5×. Elsewhere: damped 3-year CAGR (capped at 35 %/yr, decaying 10 %/yr) — the mode used is labelled on every dashboard.
  • Chargers: official network targets interpolate geometrically (Germany: 800 k AC / 200 k DC); otherwise the last three years' mean net additions continue linearly.
  • Build-out ticket: additional plugs = max(required − planned, 0), split 80/20 AC/DC; capex, revenue at scenario utilisation (capped by the unserved gap), contribution margin and simple payback are adjustable planning heuristics, not quotes.

Duty does not apply to points that are free of charge · ISO 15118-2 mandatory since 08 Jan 2026 for
new/renovated public points; ISO 15118-20 from 01 Jan 2027 — Delegated Reg (EU) 2025/656 (informational chip,
hardware-dependent, not modelled as a cost line)
  • Terminal capex €4,000 per terminal (hardware + install + integration) — heuristic. Separate stele solutions (Hectronic-type) serve whole sites; Eichrecht pairing (PTB Baumusterprüfbescheinigung) can add integration cost — retrofits are often more complex than expected.
  • Service + data fee €300/yr per terminal — heuristic midpoint: PayOne-type data fee ≈ €150/yr plus service; generic terminal fixed costs ≈ €13–25/mo (IHK).
  • Transaction fee 2.0% of ad-hoc turnover — PayOne ≈ 2% of monthly charger turnover (Mobility House FAQ). Scoped to the ad-hoc share only; contract/roaming volume does not run over the terminal.
  • Replacement cycle 4 yrs — card-scheme rules force terminal replacement roughly every 4 years (electrive practitioner analysis); modelled as a recurring capex lump on the 10-year cash curve.
  • Charge points per terminal: 1 (default) — editable; AFIR explicitly allows one terminal to serve several points of a pool, so allocated cost = terminal cost ÷ points served.
  • THG cross-note: UBA now charges fees for electricity-quantity certifications — small sites should check net THG proceeds (M3E/emobilitaet.online, 2026).
  • Permalink mapping (hash segment 4, v3.1): x flag bits = pubAccess·4 + preAfir·2 + tenvOrSecure·1 · t terminal cost line (0 off / 1 on / 2 auto) · c terminal capex € · k points served · o service fee €/yr · e transaction fee ×10 · l replacement cycle yrs · y retrofit year −2000. Data & refresh read before citing

Refresh cadence — a deliberate choice: fleet and charger statistics move monthly at best (KBA monthly, Bundesnetzagentur periodic, Eurostat/EAFO monthly-to-annual), so the GitHub Action runs monthly; a 12-hour cadence is already wired into the workflow as a commented line for the day live price feeds join the panel. Faster polling of annual statistics would only burn CI minutes, not add information.

Credits & lineage

Model architecture, formulas, constants and the Validated-Logic principle come from the author's Strategic Investment Planner: Germany 2030 (Tableau, case study for a management consultancy). The statistics engine of the master thesis E-Mobility Market Dynamics (RWTH Aachen University × Numbat GmbH, 2024) remains available as an MIT-licensed library in js/model.js; its headline finding — home-charging access drives adoption — lives on in the Urban-Business-Intensity slider. The Standort-Check tab carries German-market defaults sourced from BDEW, Elvah/Automobilwoche, NAV §11 and the 2026 THG-quota market; all are editable planning heuristics, not advice. ChargeGap Europe is an independent open-source project; the open-data, AFIR-aware product framing is inspired by live-data tools like ChargeIntel (chargeintel.eu), with which this project is not affiliated — ChargeIntel streams live CPO ad-hoc prices, ChargeGap models investment scenarios and market dynamics.
