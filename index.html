Read this honestly limitations

Thirteen of fourteen series are an indicative snapshot, so treat coefficients as a methodology demonstration until you refresh the data. Registrations are proxied by net stock change (ignores scrappage/exports). Wallbox EVs = BEV stock × a time-invariant home-charging-possible share — a documented proxy for the thesis variable, which used household-level data. The constructed price paths share one trend, so including both AC and DC price inflates their VIFs (~30): their coefficients share power and should not be read individually — the same multicollinearity caveat the original study reports for its infrastructure variables.

Original study: E-Mobility Market Dynamics (RWTH Aachen × Numbat GmbH, 2024) — 14 countries, R² = 0.735, wallbox access r = 0.795 (strongest driver), charging price r ≈ 0. This tab re-implements its quantitative track (Pearson + standardised OLS + VIF) in dependency-free JavaScript, now with a configurable panel.

Projection rules 2026–2030

  • Fleet: where an official national 2030 target exists (Germany: 15 M, Masterplan Ladeinfrastruktur II) the path interpolates geometrically to it, then the Adoption-Speed slider scales it 0.5–1.5×. Elsewhere: damped 3-year CAGR (capped at 35 %/yr, decaying 10 %/yr) — the mode used is labelled on every dashboard.
  • Chargers: official network targets interpolate geometrically (Germany: 800 k AC / 200 k DC); otherwise the last three years' mean net additions continue linearly.
  • Build-out ticket: additional plugs = max(required − planned, 0), split 80/20 AC/DC; capex, revenue at scenario utilisation (capped by the unserved gap), contribution margin and simple payback are adjustable planning heuristics, not quotes.

Data & refresh read before citing

Refresh cadence — a deliberate choice: fleet and charger statistics move monthly at best (KBA monthly, Bundesnetzagentur periodic, Eurostat/EAFO monthly-to-annual), so the GitHub Action runs monthly; a 12-hour cadence is already wired into the workflow as a commented line for the day live price feeds join the panel. Faster polling of annual statistics would only burn CI minutes, not add information.

Credits & lineage

Model architecture, formulas, constants and the Validated-Logic principle come from the author's Strategic Investment Planner: Germany 2030 (Tableau, case study for a management consultancy). The analytics track re-implements the quantitative methodology of the master thesis E-Mobility Market Dynamics — Drivers, Regulations, and Infrastructure Analysis (RWTH Aachen University × Numbat GmbH, 2024). ChargeGap Europe is an independent open-source project; the open-data, AFIR-aware product framing is inspired by live-data tools like ChargeIntel (chargeintel.eu), with which this project is not affiliated — ChargeIntel streams live CPO ad-hoc prices, ChargeGap models investment scenarios and market dynamics.
